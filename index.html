Gift of the Givers Foundation

Africa's largest independent humanitarian organisation restoring dignity to the most vulnerable.

The Gift of the Givers Foundation, a disaster response and hunger alleviation NGO, distributing aid to a community following a dam collapse in South Africa in 2022.

Humanitarian work

The Gift of the Givers Foundation was Founded in South Africa by Dr. Imtiaz Sooliman after a life-changing visit to Istanbul, Turkey

The foundation aims to reach people worldwide and their humanitarian work includes:

  • Disaster responseprovision of temporary shelter, blankets, food, medical supplies, generators, search and rescue teams, and assistance with rebuilding
  • Hunger alleviation distribution of food parcels, provision of hot meals, and agricultural assistance
  • Water provisionprovision water tankers and conduct borehole drilling operations
  • Healthcarefunding operations and provision healthcare assistance
  • Educationdevelopment of school infrastructure, provision of bursaries and small business training at schools
  • Human developmentprovision of counseling, life skills training, workshops, and skills development opportunities

  • 2001 IndiaAssisted with search and rescue operations, distributed supplies, and helped rebuild houses following an earthquake.
  • 2002 AfghanistanDistributed food, water, medicine, and tents following war and a devastating earthquake.
  • 2005 PakistanDelivered R11 million in aid and provided ongoing medical care following a cataclysmic earthquake.
  • 2008 SudanProvided urgently needed medicines and mattresses, clothing, shoes, mosquito nets, and food parcels following violence and heavy rain.
  • 2010 PortugalDispatched aid packages of food, water, diapers, pots, stoves, and heaters following a double earthquake.
  • 2010 HaitiSent a search and rescue team, disaster response specialists, equipment, and aid following a devastating earthquake.
  • 2012 SomaliaSent over 175 tons of emergency aid, and medical personnel and set up a feeding programme in response to catastrophic famine.
  • 2017 Mozambique Facilitated the delivery of a massive aid package following tropical storm Dineo.
  • 2019 South AfricaProvided water, food parcels, animal feed, and drilled boreholes and well points in response to drought.
  • 2020 LebabonProvided financial and facilitated aid distribution despite the global Covid-19 lockdown.
  • 2022 UkraineProvided remote disaster intervention training, assisted with delivery of essential goods, and provided support in response to war.
  • 2023 MalawiIntervened with aid packages, safe drinking water solutions, and provided meals following cyclone Freddy.
